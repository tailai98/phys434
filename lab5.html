The data was simulated based on real data from the ATLAS Experiment at the Large Hadron Collider at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV. The data also included probable Higgs boson data as well as the background QCD data. The data itself is measurements of the jets that are emitted during proton-proton collisions. These jets are made up of beams of particles are are a result of the high speed proton-proton collison.

The histograms shown above describe the eta value in the experiment, which is pseudorapidity. This value represents the angle from the beam at which the jets are created and seen by the detector. A small eta value corresponds with being close to perpendicular from the proton beam and a large eta value corresponds with a smaller angle between the jet and the proton beam. Seen clearly in both histograms is a normal distribution, although the Higgs eta value plots shows a smaller standard deviation than the QCD background plot.

Notice how there is not much difference between count probabilities as the phi value changes, thus meaning that this variable does not correlate with any other variable in the experiment.
